Part 1 · Defining the Foundation

What does "village" mean to you?

Village is a community of connection: a web of mutually supportive relationships, role models, shared meals, tea, fireside gatherings, dance, play, and co-creation. It is family-style support, shared vision, spaces for play, healing, movement, exploration, and art. It is a way of living closer to how we were meant to live.
The vision includes building a dance community, being a hub for playful creativity, and cultivating a sustainable food forest with sober daytime dance parties, family dance events, and traditions that attract open-minded people.

What ingredients matter most?

Dancing and music, intergenerational presence, love of children, alternative and homeschooling, creativity, open-mindedness, playfulness, presence, stewardship, sustainability, low drama, self responsibility, health, movement, healing, integrity, and gift economies.

Location and governance considerations

  • Anchor close to home while clarifying roles and agreements.
  • Explore stewardship models that honor shared space ownership.

People, roles, and contributions

  • Craftspeople, builders, and fixers
  • Artists, performers, musicians
  • Teachers and mentors
  • Grandparents and elders
  • Family members and supportive friends

Specific invitations

  • Sisters, mom, and extended family
  • Daniel's parents and friends near and far
  • Close friends including Elle, Danny, and Rose
  • Anyone aligned who can help bring shared visions to life

Conflict agreements

Commit to addressing challenges verbally instead of by text. Clarify agreements up front, with verbal confirmations and written notes when needed.

Shared agreements and commitments

Develop overarching agreements tied to the shared vision, alongside practical commitments for anyone staying on the land.

Village through the eyes of the kids

Children learn gardening, sustainability, building, music, and arts. They receive intergenerational mentorship, spend more time with grandparents and aunties, and enjoy dance parties, plays, and shared celebrations. The village nourishes with love, resilience, and belonging, while giving parents space to collaborate and rest.
Kids contribute by sharing love, laughter, curiosity, and growing into helpful, giving community members.

Current village elements

Support from mother's helpers, nanny Gianna, visiting friends, and extended family staying connected through calls and visits. Past communal experiences offered a glimpse of what worked alongside lessons about agreements and expectations.

What feels missing?

Nearby family, regular connection with dad, helpers for projects, more communal meals, co-working energy, and kindred spirits who love to dance, learn, and create.

Learning goals

  • Attracting aligned people
  • Crafting and sustaining agreements
  • Handling transitions when things shift
  • Creating learning and schooling communities
  • Exploring currency and exchange models

Part 2 · Day in the Village of Your Dreams

Envision a day surrounded by community: morning walks, shared projects in the garden and dance studio, music, meals, and planning sessions over breakfast. Family and friends drop in to collaborate, mentor the kids, and kindle creativity.

Spaces hum with sustainable living, art, and celebration. Dinnertime becomes a ritual of stories around the fire, projects progress across the property, and teachers flow in and out to support the children's curiosity.

Part 3 · Potential Obstacles

Voicing the "yeah, but..." considerations

How many people feel manageable? What about navigating drama while honoring a need for personal space? Past communal living brought joy and connection, yet highlighted the importance of clear expectations.
Concerns include judgment stemming from public past experiences, finding common ground locally, and ensuring gatherings attract aligned, intentional participants.
